#citystate

Demonym: Ashenless 

**Government Type:** Federation
The land is predominantly covered in volcanically-active land. The weather is usually warm and sunny.

**Population:** 61,096,945 (61.1 Million approx)  
(50% male, 50% female) 

**Population Density:** 65 people per sq mile (Sparse) 

**Life expectancy:** Male: 47 years, Female: 50 years 

**Average children per family:** 2

**Population Density:** 42% urban, 43% suburban, 15% rural. 

**Ethnic Groups:** 24% Natives, 59% Recent settlers, 15% Local-origin migrants, 2% Refugees 

**Languages:** There are 4 official languages, with 6 others spoken.  

**Religious Beliefs:** 11% Major religion, 32% Minor religion, 12% Agnostic, 45% Atheist.

# Military 
**Total Military Personnel:** 1,861,179 (3.0% of the total population). 

**Active Military:** 467,967 (0.77% of the total population). 

**Maximum Military Manpower:** 7,541,240 (12.34% of the total population). _This is the total amount of able bodied citizens who could take up arms if necessary, regardless of age, sex and military experience._ 

Their nation is seen as a military pushover. 

They have a defensive pact with 1 other country ([[1. World Almanac/Yetora/The Far West/City States/The Azure Sultanate\|The Azure Sultanate]]). 

Their military equipment is effective. Military service is not mandatory.
# Culture
**Unemployment rate:** 5% of the population is unemployed. 
**Poverty rate:** 7% of the population lives in poverty. 
**Literacy rate:** 99% of the population can read and write.
**Resources:** Their primary exports are Limestone, Cattle, Cotton, Corn, Gold, Stone, and Platinum.

They are well known for their Renaissance era clothing, their vocal style which features excessive vocal fry, distinctive accent, furniture and building styles.

They wear a lot of fur.  
Melodic chants and call-and-response singing are integral to Ashenless songs.  
They have a raspy accent.  
In certain regions, furniture may lack durability and quality.  
The Ashenless building style is distinctive,  
Their people are often stereotyped as being guarded.
